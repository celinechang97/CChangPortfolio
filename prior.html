How might we help college students prioritize their tasks better so that they become more efficient?

To answer this question, my teammates and I created a web application to help college students organize and prioritize their tasks in daily, weekly, and monthly views. This is different from a normal calendar in that it is targeted to help people get things done. This platform guides users by breaking down their progress - it shows when they plan on completing the task, how long it will take them to complete it, the importance of the task, and they can also mark it as done when they have accomplished it. As seen below, each card holds all the necessary information mentioned above, with colors representing the importance to help users visualize priority – the darker the shade of red, the higher the priority of the task.

how we started
Students lead busy lives – balancing between school, work, extracurricular and social activities. With such a busy schedule, it is critical that they stay on top of their responsibilities. However, it can be tough to know where to start.
interviews & observations
Students’ biggest pain of their current modes of organization was
difficult accessibility and usage.
They spent too much time organizing the tasks than actually executing them as it didn't actually help them prioritize all the tasks. Moreover, many people used multiple methods of organization to keep track of all the different tasks in their lives, which was inefficient as they had to navigate back and forth between their applications.
prototyping
Before implementing the application on a digital platform, we started with paper prototyping and user-testing with the mock-up to ensure that we hit all of the pains and needs we found through our interviews and observations. We constantly conducted user-testing throughout our process even as we moved onto the digital platform and implemented those valuable feedback in our final design.
features of final design
❮
An empty calendar when you first start.
We made the process of creating tasks quick and easy as it only contains the essentials -- name, type, time, and importance.
Once they are created, we made it easy to take in the information by creating “cards” per task, which contain all the information the user put in when they created the task. .
Users can mark the task as done, edit, and delete as they wish to.
The weekly view has the same information as the daily view, but because there are more days and items to be displayed on the interface, only the name and the type(icon) are presented. The user can get more details when they hover over the task. For example, the black blurb under “Friend’s Birthday” appears.
The monthly view has the same information as the daily view, but because there are more days and items to be displayed on the interface, only the name is presented. The user can get more detials when they hover over the task. For example, the black blurb under “NUSOLAR.”
In all three views, the user can filter by type - class, work, social, and other. The application shows all the tasks by default.
