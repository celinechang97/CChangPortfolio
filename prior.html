prior
 
For a Human Computer Interaction course, my teammates and I created a web application to help college students organize and prioritize their tasks in daily, weekly, and monthly views. As seen below, each card holds all the necessary information regarding the task, with colors representing the importance to help users visualize priority -- the darker the shade of red, the higher the priority of the task.

how we started
Students lead busy lives -- balancing between school, work, extracurricular and social activities. It is critical that they stay on top of their responsibilities, but it can be tough to know where to start.
interviews & observations
We found the students’ biggest pain point of their current modes of organization to be
difficulty with accessibility and ease of use.
They spent way too much time organizing the tasks than actually executing those tasks. Most used multiple modes of organization to keep track of all the different events in their lives, which was inefficient as they had to navigate back and forth between their applications.
prototyping
To address our users’ biggest pain point, we made sure to make this application accessible by making it into a web application that can be easily accessed from computers-- something students always carry around. In addition, we ensured that the process of navigating and utilizing this app was as easy as possible by keeping everything minimal.
features of final design
❮
We made the process of creating tasks quick and easy as it only contains the essentials -- name, type, time, and importance.
Once they are created, we made it easy to take in the information by creating “cards” per task, which contain all the information the user put in when they created the task. .
Users can mark the task as done, edit, and delete as they wish to.
The weekly view has the same information as the daily view, but because there are more days and items to be displayed on the interface, only the name and the type(icon) are presented. The user can get more details when they hover over the task. For example, the black blurb under “Friend’s Birthday” appears.
The monthly view has the same information as the daily view, but because there are more days and items to be displayed on the interface, only the name is presented. The user can get more detials when they hover over the task. For example, the black blurb under “NUSOLAR.”
In all three views, the user can filter by type - class, work, social, and other. The application shows all the tasks by default.
