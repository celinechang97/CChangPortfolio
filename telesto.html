How might we help college students live a
less stressful
life?

For my User Experience class, my teammates and I designed a phone application to help college students be on track in a mindful and non-stressful way — to not only focus on the tasks they have to do, but also to be aware of when they are free. Thus, tasks, including free time, are represented by different colors. Along with this value, we encouraged mindfulness by framing tasks as accomplishments — to see a task as part of a process of a long journey. Therefore, we designed the calendar itself to be a universe, a space to be explored, with the tasks (circles) representing planets to be conquered.

how we started
Because students lead such busy lives, it’s easy to get caught up in the midst of everything, making it easy to forget to breathe for a second. Even when they have free time, they constantly double check to make sure that they did everything — not being able to take full advantage of the time to relax.
insights from interviews & observations
Our biggest finding was that
people were constantly rechecking their planners.
They were stress-looking to ensure that they didn’t have any commitments they had overlooked. This was because they didn’t have a clear marker to indicate when they actually could take a break.
prototyping, user-testing, & iterating
Wanting to emphasize free time and a perspective of treating tasks as accomplishments, we initially designed an interface drastically different from other calendars. The circles represented tasks, with colors distinguishing the subject, and were used to emphasize the planets the user would be conquering as they accomplished their tasks. The dotted lines represented the time they had in between the two tasks.
However, through user testing, we found that the floating circles and the trails were not intuitive enough for the users. For example, the length of the trail wasn't clear enough to know how much time they had left.
Therefore, we made a quick pivot.
