slick
 
For a Design Thinking and Doing course, my teammates and I created a portable desk to help students be as productive and efficient as they want to be while not comprising comfort. As the weather gets warmer, many students take advantage of the sun by working outside. However, most, if not all, students find it difficult to find the perfect position as they try to take notes, use laptops, or read textbooks. Therefore, we wanted to help them soak in vitamin D while they got their work done in a comfortable position by using our portable desk. The surface is big enough to hold their laptop or notebook, but small and light enough to be carried around in their backpacks.

how we started
With so much on students’ plates, it’s important to utilize every bit of time they can get anywhere, anytime. However, those environments are not suitable to get real work done because, for example, they lack a hard surface to work on.
interviews & observations
We initially started with the goal of making public transportation rides more efficient for students because those long commutes could be used productively if the environment allowed it.
However, we found that there was a broader problem:
Difficult for people to be productive outside in general -- in a non-traditional desk setting.
prototyping
Because users were going to be carrying this product, it was important to make it as compact and light as possible.
People had different preferences for the height of the desk. Therefore, we utilized a magnetic strip on the side to make it slide and adjust to fit our users’ comfort.
features of final design
❮
We made the process of creating tasks quick and easy as it only contains the essentials -- name, type, time, and importance.
Once they are created, we made it easy to take in the information by creating “cards” per task, which contain all the information the user put in when they created the task. .
Users can mark the task as done, edit, and delete as they wish to.
The weekly view has the same information as the daily view, but because there are more days and items to be displayed on the interface, only the name and the type(icon) are presented. The user can get more details when they hover over the task. For example, the black blurb under “Friend’s Birthday” appears.
The monthly view has the same information as the daily view, but because there are more days and items to be displayed on the interface, only the name is presented. The user can get more detials when they hover over the task. For example, the black blurb under “NUSOLAR.”
In all three views, the user can filter by type - class, work, social, and other. The application shows all the tasks by default.
