unknot
 
For an extracurricular activity, my friend and I designed a phone application to create a platform for those that used to write for fun but don’t as much anymore because they simply don’t have time and feel unmotivated to do so. We created an app where the users receive two themes everyday to write about in any form they want to and share it with whomever they choose to.

how we started
Mark wanted to study journalism in high school and planned on pursuing it when he came to college. However, in light of the reality that it’s harder to succeed with a journalism major, he switched to computer science. Ever since he switched, he stopped writing. Even though he missed it greatly, he simply didn’t have the time and the motivation to continue.
prototyping
Writing can be difficult. It’s easy to stare at a blank document for hours, not knowing where to start. Therefore, we decided to add a feature of sending themes and inspirational quotes to the writers to help them get started.
We wanted this platform to be their getaway where they could solely immerse themselves in their writing. We didn’t want our users to be defined or judged by anything other than writing. Therefore, although it is a social platform in a way, we refrained from exposing any personal information.
features of final design
❮
We named our app unknot and chose our logo to be a loose yarnball to give a sense of relaxation. Moreover, we wanted our users to take that loose end and run free with it, unraveling the expertise they have instored as they write freely.
Users will receive a theme twice a day -- 6:30 AM and 8:30 PM. These times were specifically chosen because those are the times people either get ready for work or get off work and get on a long commute on public trasnportation.
Along with the theme, an inspirational quote related to the theme will be sent.
All of the themes received will be in this section with different icons:
- open envelope: read
- open envelope with paper: started writing, but not published
- closed envelope: published
Once they click on a theme, the app will take them to a page of posts made by other users.
If they want to write about the theme, they can click on the “+” button on the bottom right corner.
This is the user’s priofile page. It keeps all the themes they have written about.
This is all the posts of other writers that the user saved.
